{\bfseries DEVELOPMENT OF A COMPANIONSHIP CHATBOT WITH CONVERSATIONAL DEPRESSION SCREENING AND HYBRID KNOWLEDGE-GRAPH-BASED LONG-TERM MEMORY FOR INDONESIAN UNIVERSITY STUDENTS}
\end{center}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\begin{center}
By \\
Mohammad Nugraha Eka Prawira \\
NIM: 13522001
\end{center}

\vspace{0.5cm}

Indonesian university students face academic and emotional pressure, while professional support is not always readily accessible. This thesis develops AXIS, a nonclinical companionship chatbot combining reflective dialogue based on Cognitive Behavioral Therapy principles, conversational mood checks and PHQ-9 depression screening, and hybrid long-term memory using a knowledge graph and semantic search. PHQ-9 is positioned as screening rather than diagnosis. Evaluation uses contract validation, labelled benchmarks, blind LLM-as-judge assessment, scenario-and-outcome testing, and paired ablation. A total of 54 CBT dialogue contracts, 80 safety contracts, 130 PHQ-9 contracts, and 47 memory contracts passed. Across 24 blind dialogue scenarios, AXIS was preferred 14 times, but agreement between judge configurations was low, with Cohen's kappa of $-0.035$. Safety detection achieved sensitivity of 0.654 and specificity of 0.917. Of 80 free-text PHQ-9 answers, 73 unambiguous answers matched the reference labels, while seven ambiguous answers triggered clarification. Graph writing achieved node F1 of 0.620 and relation F1 of 0.361, while lifecycle action correctness reached only 33.3\%. On 42 queries, hybrid retrieval improved P@5, MRR, nDCG@5, and relational query success, but did not show a consistent improvement in Recall@5 or answer-level memory usage. These results support prototype engineering feasibility and a limited benefit of graph structure for retrieval ranking and specific relational cases, rather than general superiority, clinical effectiveness, or validated user experience.

\noindent {\bfseries Keywords:} companionship chatbot, depression screening, PHQ-9, hybrid long-term memory, knowledge graph, Indonesian university students
